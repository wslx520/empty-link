Empty link means, when click on the link, nothing will happen.

  • Don't flashjump to the top of the page
  • Don't change current page's location
  • Don't open new page

It's simple ,but because of many costomed browser or old browser, it might become not easy.

some common ways to get a empty link:

  • href="javascript:;"
  • href="javascript:void 0;"
  • href="javascript:void(0);"
  • href="#!"

There are some examples below, click on them to check.

Under simple test(by me, very careless), if the link has a `target="_blank"`, all above ways will open a new page(except Chrome, Chrome just open new page when `target="_blank" href="#!"`

So, let me show a conclusion:

  • If you are NOT IN a `front-end route` system, use `#!` please.
  • If you are IN a `front-end route` system, use `javascript:` please.
  • NEVER add `target` to your empty link
